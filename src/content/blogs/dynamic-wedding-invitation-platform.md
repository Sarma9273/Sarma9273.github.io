## Introduction

Traditional wedding invitations are beautiful, but they provide limited space for event details, family introductions, photographs, directions, and updates.

I worked on a dynamic wedding invitation platform that could serve as a digital extension of a traditional invitation. The objective was to create a personalised website that family members and invited guests could access from their phones.

The platform was designed not as a generic template, but as a carefully organised digital celebration.

## Website Structure

The invitation platform includes sections for:

- Main invitation

- Bride and groom introduction

- Wedding-event details

- Muhurtham information

- Family members

- Bride’s siblings and cousins

- Groom’s siblings and cousins

- Photo collections

- Location information

- Navigation between major sections

Each event can include a title, description, date, time, venue, cover image, and supporting gallery.

## Event Presentation

One important design decision was to use a dedicated thumbnail or cover image for each event instead of automatically displaying a collage of all images.

This gives each event a cleaner visual identity and helps guests understand what the section represents before opening it.

The event descriptions are also displayed clearly so that the images do not appear without context.

## Navigation Experience

The navigation was designed to guide visitors through the invitation without making the page feel locked or repetitive.

After opening the invitation, users should be able to explore the content and return to the main content area naturally.

Unnecessary navigation arrows and elements that might expose technical or source-related information were removed. The focus remained entirely on the invitation experience.

## Light and Dark Presentation

The platform was tested with different visual themes.

When switching between dark and light modes, the card backgrounds, text colours, dividers, and decorative elements must remain readable and visually balanced.

This required more than changing the main background colour. Each component had to be reviewed to ensure that the design remained consistent.

## Content Organisation

The photographs and event images were organised into source folders with clear filenames.

This is particularly important for a family-oriented website because new images may need to be added later. A predictable folder structure makes it easier to update the invitation without changing the underlying code.

The project also included decisions about naming specific images, such as the wedding-muhurtham card, event-cover photographs, and family-category thumbnails.

## Privacy Considerations

Because the invitation is intended for invited family members and friends, privacy and discoverability were considered during the design.

The public-facing experience should not display unnecessary developer instructions, code references, or internal notes.

The website must feel like a polished invitation rather than a development project.

## Challenges and Learning

This project helped me understand that frontend development is not only about writing code. Small details such as image selection, navigation behaviour, text placement, colour contrast, and mobile responsiveness strongly affect the user’s experience.

It also taught me how to translate family expectations and visual preferences into practical website components.

## Key takeaway
The wedding invitation platform gave me practical experience in building a highly personalised web application. It helped me improve frontend design, content organisation, responsive development, user navigation, image management, and attention to emotional and cultural details.
